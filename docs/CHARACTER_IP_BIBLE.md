# Sunny Spoon Village - Character IP Bible

## Purpose

This is the continuity contract for games, chapters, animation, books, promotional media, merchandise, and licensed work set in Sunny Spoon Village.

It preserves the character identity already visible in the game. It does not claim that a common name, animal species, clothing item, or broad cozy-art convention is legally exclusive.

## Status Language

- `In-game baseline`: approved for continuity with the current v1.36 game.
- `Franchise master pending`: needs a final turnaround, palette sheet, expression sheet, and human art approval before broad licensing or merchandise production.
- `Clearance pending`: needs name, trademark, and visual-similarity review before a major commercial expansion.

All current characters are an in-game baseline. Elena, Pip, Aunt Mina, and the Sunny Spoon Village marks remain franchise master pending and clearance pending.

## Franchise Identity

The defensible identity is the combination of:

`Sunny Spoon Village + Elena's Dream Studio + Pip the capybara helper + relationship memories + practical cozy village problems`

Do not present the property as a generic restaurant simulator, generic animal village, or lesson app with decorative characters.

## Core Character Rules

### Elena

- Story function: active protagonist, junior cafe helper, and future Dream Studio designer.
- Visual anchors: side-part bob, heart clip, mint work apron, sketchbook.
- Behavioral anchors: notices details, makes spaces welcoming, sketches what could exist next.
- Emotional range: curiosity, uncertainty, concentration, confidence, and quiet pride.
- Voice: warm, observant, and practical; never a teacher proxy.
- Non-negotiable: Elena makes the meaningful choice. Pip can clue, react, and comfort but does not replace her agency.

### Pip

- Story function: capybara companion, clue carrier, mascot, and emotional anchor.
- Visual anchors: round low capybara silhouette, small ears and muzzle, tiny bow, soft smile.
- Behavioral anchors: sniffs, points, holds cards, finds warm resting spots, celebrates gently.
- Voice: short sounds, signs, and body language; very limited full sentences.
- Emotional rhythm: calm, loyal, quietly funny, and reassuring rather than chaotic.
- Non-negotiable: Pip stays pet-like and recognizably capybara-shaped. Do not redesign him as a bear, hamster, generic plush mascot, or loud talking adult.

### Aunt Mina

- Story function: cafe owner, family mentor, and keeper of recipe memories.
- Visual anchors: side bun, mentor apron, wooden spoon, well-used recipe book.
- Behavioral anchors: makes room for Elena to try, admits when she needs help, thanks people specifically.
- Voice: encouraging and concise; she introduces real needs instead of assigning lessons.
- Non-negotiable: Aunt Mina is not a generic tutorial narrator or endless quest dispenser.

## Visual Production Contract

- Preserve each character's face, silhouette, signature prop, and dominant palette across assets.
- Small UI portraits use the dedicated headshot atlas. Never crop a full-body sticker into a face container.
- Full-body art is reserved for scenes, Lookbook review, promotional key art, and approved collectible presentations.
- Outfit variants may change clothing and accessories but must preserve face, hair silhouette, body proportion, and core prop language.
- Expression variants may exaggerate emotion but must not change age, species, facial structure, or identifying marks.
- Pip accessories must remain small enough that his capybara silhouette reads first.
- Generated, commissioned, or licensed art must be checked against the approved in-game baseline before integration.

## Writing And Content Contract

- Every return appearance should reveal a preference, relationship change, worry, promise, memory, or aspiration.
- Educational content may use a character's situation, but a character must not collapse into a curriculum category.
- Repeated missions should vary emotional reason, visitor, occasion, object state, or resulting keepsake.
- Character conflict stays cozy: misunderstanding, uncertainty, limited time, missing objects, competing preferences, or a promise that needs care.
- New media may widen the world, but Elena, Pip, and Aunt Mina keep the same agency hierarchy and emotional roles.

## Franchise Expansion Gate

Before a sequel, book, animated short, merchandise line, or public licensing package:

1. Approve front/side/back character turnarounds for Elena, Pip, and Aunt Mina.
2. Approve fixed color palettes and monochrome silhouettes.
3. Approve expression and action sheets that cover each character's emotional range.
4. Run visual-similarity review against relevant existing properties.
5. Run name and trademark clearance in intended markets and product categories.
6. Record asset provenance, creator agreements, and commercial-use rights.
7. Add approved derivatives to this document without replacing the baseline history.

## Current Assessment

Pip is the strongest standalone mascot candidate. Elena and Sunny Spoon Village are strongest as part of the combined world identity. The current cast is suitable for continued game development, but no character should be described as legally cleared or commercially exclusive until the expansion gate is complete.
